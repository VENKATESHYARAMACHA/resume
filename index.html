Academic projects:

  1. Optimal design of a new Multilevel inverter using reduced number of power electronic components

In Multilevel inverter as the number of levels are increasing, the number of power electronic components used will be increase. So the losses will increase. In this project we have designed a new multilevel inverter which give a better output in terms of number of levels with reduced number of power electronic elements compared to conventional multilevel converters. The switching pulses for the switches is given by FPGA programming. The circuit is designed on MATLAB software. Hard ware is also executed.

  1. Comparative Study of PV based converter topologies for pumping applications

Now-a-days usage of renewable energy sources increasing drastically because of scarcity of non-renewable energy sources like coal, fuel etc. One of the popular renewable energy sources is solar. Out of generated energy maximum amount of energy is consumed by motors and pumps. So if we use renewable energy for these we can save lot of energy.

In this project, comparative study of DC-DC converter topologies like (SEPIC, LANDSMAN and ZETA converters) has been done. Finally we summarized which converter is giving better performance.
